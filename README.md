# Use-of-Sentiment-Analysis-to-Uncover-Product-Risks-at-Beats-by-Dre
From Reviews to Strategy: How I Used Sentiment Analysis to Uncover Product Risks at Beats by Dre

**Author:** Tobou Egbekun
**Focus:** Customer Perception & Revenue Risk Analysis
**Stack:** Python (NLP), AI-Assisted Analysis, Competitive Benchmarking

---

## Overview

In saturated consumer markets, brand strength alone is not enough to sustain growth.

This project analyzes large-scale customer sentiment across the true wireless earbuds category to answer a critical business question:

> **Where does the brand win emotionally — and where does it lose functionally?**

Using NLP-driven sentiment analysis and competitive benchmarking, this study identifies hidden experience gaps that impact customer trust, retention, and long-term product positioning.

---

## The Problem

Most consumer brands rely on:

* Star ratings
* Surface-level reviews
* Brand perception

But these signals hide a deeper issue:

> **Customers can love a brand — and still not trust the product.**

In the $100–$150 earbuds segment, competitors are closing the gap with:

* Better durability
* Improved battery life
* Stronger functional performance

This creates a **silent risk**:
Customers remain emotionally attached, but begin to question value.

---

## Core Insight

> **Brand loyalty does not eliminate functional expectations.**

When product experience falls short, trust erodes — even if sentiment remains positive.

---

## Methodology

### Data Collection

* 1,000+ verified customer reviews
* Multiple products across leading competitors
* Public e-commerce and review platforms

---

### Analytical Approach

**1. Text Preprocessing (Python NLP)**

* Cleaning, tokenization, stopword removal

**2. Sentiment Analysis**

* Classification of positive, neutral, and negative sentiment
* Aggregation at brand and feature level

**3. Theme Extraction**

* Clustering feedback into key experience dimensions:

  * Fit & comfort
  * Battery life
  * Controls
  * Durability
  * Price perception

**4. Competitive Benchmarking**

* Cross-brand comparison across identical themes

**5. Insight Structuring**

* SCQA Framework
* Pyramid Principle (answer-first communication)

---

## Key Findings

### 1. Strength — Brand Identity & Tactile Control

Customers consistently value:

* Signature sound profile
* Recognizable design
* Physical button controls

> **Tactile feedback is a real competitive advantage**, especially for active users.

---

### 2. Gap — Durability & Stability

A consistent negative sentiment trend (~25–30%) revealed:

* Earbuds slipping during workouts
* Weak perceived build quality
* Battery concerns relative to price

> These are not isolated complaints — they are repeated patterns.

---

### 3. Strategic Risk — Price vs Value Perception

At the $100–$150 range, customers compare against feature-heavy competitors offering:

* Better fit stability
* Longer battery life
* Stronger durability

> **The risk is not losing attention — it is losing trust.**

---

## Strategic Recommendations

### 1. Improve Fit & Stability

* Introduce enhanced ear-tip design (e.g., wing-tips)
* Target high-movement use cases

**Impact:** Reduced churn, stronger product reliability perception

---

### 2. Reinforce Durability

* Upgrade case hinge quality
* Improve material finish

**Impact:** Increased perceived value and pricing justification

---

### 3. Position Physical Controls as Premium

* Market tactile buttons as a performance feature
* Differentiate from touch-based competitors

**Impact:** Clear positioning in an overcrowded category

---

## Final Insight

> **Customers do not leave because they stop liking the brand.**
> **They leave because they stop trusting the product.**

Closing the gap between **emotional appeal and functional performance** is what determines long-term category leadership.
